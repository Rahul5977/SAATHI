The reasoning chain has eight components. Read all constraints carefully — every field maps directly to the dialogue generation rules the conversation was built under.


COMPONENT DEFINITIONS


── 1. STIMULUS ──
The specific Indian social/cultural trigger behind the Seeker's
current state. Two parts:

  event    : What happened externally, described in one sentence

  category : Resolve from the situation metadata context_category

── 2. COPING MECHANISM & SHADE ──
Identify what is active in the Seeker's current turn:

  mechanism : One of: Somatization | Sequential |
              Relational_Preservation | Duty_Based | null

              Restatement_or_Paraphrasing, identify which lens

              A=physical idiom | B=role/duty | C=relational |
              D=time/duration | E=feared consequence |
              F=emotional overwhelm (Sequential only)

── 3. STIGMA LAYER ──
How social stigma, disclosure barriers, or cultural shame shapes
what the Seeker says or withholds in this turn. Must address:
  (a) What the Seeker is NOT saying and why — reference the
      persona register (e.g. P1 buries emotion in duty language;
      P5 never names anger; P7 has no psychological vocabulary).
  (b) The specific cultural inhibitor: "log kya kahenge" /
      fear of burdening family / "mard nahi rota" /
      "mental problem nahi hai mujhe" / izzat / biradari pressure
      / other — name it explicitly.
  (c) How this distorts the surface conversation — what the
      Seeker's words suggest vs. what they actually carry.

── 4. APPRAISAL ──
The Seeker's internal interpretation of the trigger through a
collectivist lens — tied to family honour, duty, social role, or
community expectations. Must begin: "The Seeker thinks…"
Must reference at least one of: family obligation, izzat,
role identity, log kya kahenge, duty to provider role,
or community gaze.

── 5. EMOTION SHIFT ──
Transition in the Seeker's emotional state:

  previous   : Dominant emotion label from prior turns
  current    : Dominant emotion label in the current turn
               (Turn 1 must match situation metadata emotion_seed)
  intensity  : Integer 1–6. Turn 1 must match intensity_seed.
               Must decrease max 1 per Supporter turn, may increase
               1 if a Restatement opened a deeper layer.
  trajectory : escalating | de-escalating | stable | fluctuating

── 6. RISK ANALYSIS ──
Immediate safety assessment. One level + one-line justification:
  Low | Mild | Moderate | High
  (matches the risk_flag taxonomy: none→Low, mild→Mild,
   moderate→Moderate, high→High)
Justification must reference what in the Seeker's language
triggered the assessment — not a generic statement.

── 7. PHASE & STRATEGY ──
  phase    : Exploration | Insight | Action
             Exploration requires intensity ≥ 4 in JSON.
             Insight requires intensity = 3 AND core conflict named.
             Action requires intensity = 2 AND readiness signal present.
             Readiness signals: "ab kya karoon" / "koi raasta hai kya" /
             "theek hona chahta hoon" / "batao kya karoon" /
             "kuch samajh nahi aa raha aage ka"

             Restatement_or_Paraphrasing | Question |
             Reflection_of_Feelings | Affirmation_and_Reassurance |
             Self_disclosure | Providing_Suggestions |
             Execution | Information
  strategy_intent : One sentence: why this strategy was chosen +
             which lens was used (if Restatement) + what specific
             element of the Seeker's turn was mirrored or addressed.
             Must begin: "To [goal], the Supporter uses [strategy]
             to [specific action], mirroring [element] within the
             [Indian cultural context detail]."

── 8. PROHIBITION CHECK ──
Flag any violation present in the Supporter's last turn.
If none, output "none". If a violation exists, name the exact
rule from this list that was broken:

  phase_advanced_early | wrong_lens_for_mechanism |
  self_disclosure_used_twice | clinical_western_language |
